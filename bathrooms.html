Straight Bath

No, this isn't some anti-LGBTQ+ propoganda. A Straight Bath is probably the most common type of bath that attaches to your wall in your bathroom on 3 of the 4 sides, creating a sort-of "alcove look"

They are the standard in most modern Bathrooms, so if you wanna be one of the gang and a functioning member of society, or if you don't have much room to play about with in your Bathroom

Wet-Room Installation

Carpeted Bathrooms? EW!

Yeah that trend died long ago along with the boomers who suggested the rot-harvesting, mould-gathering, moist-toe-tickler type of design

So a lot of people these days opt for Wet-Rooms. One smooth floor running across the whole room, it doesn't matter if it gets wet because it all leads to the same drain.

You can think about like one of those shared changing rooms in the local swimming baths they took you in in primary school. If little TimmyTM has an accident while getting changed, well it'll just run off into the center of the room

Alternatively, think of it as One-Big-Shower. Scrub your natters over by the sink for all we care. It all flows towards the drain.

Corner Baths

You guessed it, these fit in the corner of the room.

Two sides stick to the walls of the bathroom leaving the other two free. These, like the bodies that will use them, can come in many shapes. Curved, Geometric, Box-like, some can even have the end of it come away forming a little "L-Shape".

A popular design for those who cherish their bathroom space.
